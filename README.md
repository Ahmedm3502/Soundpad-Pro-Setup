## 🛠 Features of the application

This software includes many tools to manage your audio experience:

*   **Soundboard management:** Organize your sound files into lists. You can name these lists and save them for later use.
*   **Custom hotkeys:** Assign any sound to a specific keyboard key. Pressing this key plays the sound into your microphone channel.
*   **Voice effects:** Change how you sound to others. Apply filters to increase pitch, add room reverb, or create robotic audio.
*   **Audio mixer:** Adjust the volume of your microphone and the soundboard independently. This prevents audio clipping during loud moments.
*   **Built-in recorder:** Capture audio directly from your desktop or microphone. You can edit these clips and save them for instant playback.
*   **File support:** The program supports common audio formats like MP3, WAV, and FLAC.
*   **Normalize volume:** The software forces your audio clips to a consistent level. This stops a loud sound from overwhelming your listeners.

## 🎮 Setting up the program for Discord

To use this with Discord, you must choose the correct input device:

1. Open your Discord settings.
2. Select "Voice & Video" from the left menu.
3. Change your "Input Device" to "Soundpad Microphone" or the virtual audio device the software creates.
4. Keep your "Output Device" as your normal headphones or speakers.
5. Open the Soundpad application.
6. Ensure that the "Enable microphone" option is active within the Soundpad menu.

## 💡 Usage tips for experts

You can improve your experience by following these practices:

*   **Label your sounds:** Give every file a descriptive name. This helps you find the right clip during a fast-paced game.
*   **Manage hotkey conflicts:** Check that your chosen hotkeys do not overlap with your game controls. Use specialized keys like the number pad or the function keys if necessary.
*   **Maintain file paths:** Do not move your audio files after you add them to the program. The software remembers the location of the files. If you move them, the software cannot play the sound.
*   **Adjust gain:** Use the internal gain settings to ensure your clips play at the same loudness as your physical microphone input.

## ❓ Troubleshooting common issues

If you encounter problems, check these items first:

*   **Sound does not play:** Verify that the file is in the correct format. Check the volume slider in the main window.
*   **Others cannot hear me:** Confirm that Discord uses the correct microphone device. Make sure the soundboard is not muted in the settings menu.
*   **The program feels slow:** Close unnecessary background applications that consume a large amount of system memory.
*   **Audio crackling:** Lower the sample rate in your sound settings. Higher sample rates sometimes cause issues on older audio hardware.
